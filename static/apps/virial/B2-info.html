B2 can be computed for any spherical potential via simple quadrature. Additionally, B2 for soft-spheres is more easily expressible as a constant times the gamma function and B2 for Lennard-Jones can be expressed as a series expansion of gamma functions. The faster methods are used when computing B2 for untruncated soft spheres or Lennard-Jones, while quadrature is used for all other choices. The coefficient value along with its first two derivatives with inverse temperature (β=1/kBT) are reported. When quadrature is used, the results using between 210 ≈ 103 and 220 ≈ 106 quadrature points (distnaces where the interaction potential and Mayer function are evaluated) are reported to provide a measure of convergence.
